Definition: Silence

silence, n. 2 Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪləns/ Forms: ME–15 scilence, ME–15 scylence, 15 scylens; ME cilence; ME sylens(e, 15 Sc. silens, ME–15 sylence, ME– silence. Etymology: < Old French silence, scilence, < Latin silentium, < silēre to be silent: compare Provençal silenci, Spanish silencio, Portuguese silencio, Italian silenzio 2. a. The state or condition when nothing is audible; absence of all sound or noise; complete quietness or stillness; noiselessness. Sometimes personified.
